Gabriola Island NDVI Using GEE


Gabriola Island is one of the Gulf Islands in the Strait of Georgia in British Columbia, Canada. It has a land area of about 57.6 square kilometres and a resident population of 4,500.
Remote Sensing techniques enable visualizing the geography of the island, terrain and vegetation health conditions. Google Earth Engine is a cloud-based geospatial analysis platform that enables users to visualize and analyze satellite images of our planet.
Here, I am have used latest base map imagery available for Gabriola Island, we see a portion of land in the northeast in brown color indicating lack of vegetation. Let's take a look at this portion of land using satellite imagery. Here, I am using Sentinel imagery from July 2020, creating a composite of available imagery, filtering out the clouds and visualizing the Normalized Difference Vegetation Index. On the map, darker blue represents healthy vegetation while lighter green represents no vegetation or the ocean.
I also looked at digital terrain model (DEM) and derived a hillshade, symbolized using Hard Light blend mode to visualize the terrain under a symbolized DEM.
